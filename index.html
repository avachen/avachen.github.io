About Me

Ava Chen is a graduate student at Columbia University in the Robotic Manipulation and Mobility Lab, working towards a PhD in Mechanical Engineering. She holds a BS in Mechanical Engineering from MIT, where she developed an interest in assistive and rehabilitative robotics. Her previous work includes developing instrumentation for measuring limb tissue biomechanics and studying mid-air reorientation dynamics in jumping spiders.

Her current research is focused on electromechanical development of a robotic hand orthosis that augments functional dexterity for individuals with post-stroke hemiparesis. Pivotal to this project is the integration of hardware and software engineer, physician, therapist, and stroke community involvement throughout the design process.

Ava has additional interests in adaptive technology — leveraging the potential of robotics and AI to create mutable interfaces between people and their environments — in accessible design for wearable devices, and in closing the loop between bio-inspired robotics and observational biology by using robotic principles to emulate and test hypothesized biological mechanisms.
