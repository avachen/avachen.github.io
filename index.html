I am a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Columbia University, advised by Matei Ciocarlie. I work in the Robotic Manipulation and Mobility (ROAM) Lab, where I study robotic hand orthoses for assisting and rehabilitating hemiparetic stroke survivors. My research focus is hardware design for better-fitting and force-effective wearable robots; I also study human-centered learning, physical human-robot interaction and feedback, and human-robot coordination with exoskeletons. I am supported by a NIH F31 Ruth L. Kirchstein National Research Service Award.


Before joining Columbia, I was a research assistant at Harvard University and worked with Paul Shamble. I received my Bachelors at MIT in Mechanical Engineering and was advised by Hugh Herr.

I am happy to mentor motivated Columbia MS/undergraduate students. To discuss potential research opportunities/project ideas, please reach out to me over email! Currently, I have the pleasure to work with: Alex Deli‑Ivanov, Connor Lee, Grace Munger, Akshay Venkatesan, Matheu Campbell.
